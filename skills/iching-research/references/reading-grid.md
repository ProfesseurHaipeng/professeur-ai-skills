# Reading grid

For each output, fill these headings in order:

1. **Question and scope** — rewrite the question so it is answerable without fate claims.
2. **Input and convention** — list date, place, method, edition, and uncertainty.
3. **Textual evidence** — quote briefly or paraphrase with a direct source link.
4. **Calculation** — show formulas, lookup tables, or a refusal reason.
5. **Competing readings** — include at least one alternative school or translation.
6. **Reflection** — translate symbols into questions, not instructions.
7. **Reality check** — list observable evidence and a next action.

For a “will I succeed?” prompt, replace the binary prediction with: success definition, time window, controllable actions, base-rate evidence, risks, and a review date. A symbolic reading can generate hypotheses; it cannot estimate a success probability without an outcome dataset and a declared evaluation design.
