# Evaluation protocol

To test “does it work?”, define the claim before drawing cards or reading charts:

1. Specify an outcome, time window, population, and baseline.
2. Pre-register the interpretation rule and what counts as a hit.
3. Blind the reader to outcomes and the evaluator to the reading source when possible.
4. Compare against a base-rate or shuffled-control condition.
5. Report misses, ambiguous cases, selection effects, and confidence intervals.

Do not use user testimonials as a predictive-accuracy estimate. They can document subjective usefulness, not causal validity.
